BEGINNER'S GUIDE

What is Vocaloid?

Vocaloid is a type of voice synthesizer software. A musician writes lyrics and notes, chooses a voicebank, and edits the performance to create a sung vocal track. The result can be gentle, robotic, dramatic, playful, or surprisingly human depending on the voice and the producer's choices. Our Vocaloid India Community is here to help newcomers explore it without feeling lost.

Why is Hatsune Miku so well known?

Hatsune Miku is a virtual singer character who introduced many people to Vocaloid. Her blue-haired design represents a voicebank, but the songs are made by thousands of independent Vocaloid producers. That open creative culture is why she has become part of concerts, games, animation, fan art, and music scenes around the world—including Hatsune Miku India fan spaces.

Is Vocaloid the only option?

No. Synthesizer V, UTAU, and CeVIO AI are other singing-synthesis platforms. Each has its own software, voices, and workflow. You do not need to choose one immediately: listening to Vocaloid songs and community highlights is a great way to learn what you enjoy.

Quick facts Virtual singers are instruments for creators. A character, a voicebank, and the software are related—but they are not always the same thing.

FAQ

Vocaloid questions, answered.

A short Vocaloid Wiki-style starting point for anyone discovering virtual singers and voice synthesis for the first time.

What is a voicebank?

A voicebank is the vocal data used by Vocaloid software or another voice synthesizer. It gives the finished song its singer, language, and character.

Are Vocaloid characters real singers?

Vocaloid characters are virtual singers linked to a voicebank or music project. The human voice provider, illustrator, and software company may all have different roles.

Who makes Vocaloid songs?

Vocaloid producers make songs in many genres: pop, rock, electronic music, ballads, experimental music, and more. Fans also create art, videos, translations, covers, and dance projects.

Where can I begin?

Listen widely, follow artists you like, and join a respectful Vocaloid community. Our events page is another way to find people who enjoy the same music.
